Route #1

Þjóðvegur 1

The Route 1 is a national road in Iceland that runs around the island and connects most of the inhabited parts of the country. The total length of the road is 1,332 kilometres (828 mi). Some of the most popular tourist attractions in Iceland are by the Ring Road.

This plan is a 6-day road trip that follows Route #1 and visitiing some of the most iconic sites along the way.

0 mi

The Origin: Reykjavík

Our trip starts in Reykjavík, the capital city of Iceland. Steam from hot springs in the region is said to have inspired Reykjavík's name, which loosely translates to Smoke Cove (the city is sometimes referred to as Bay of Smoke or Smoky Bay in English language travel guides).

Reykjavík to Vík

Towards the Black Beach

Leaving Reykjavík we head to the south coast towards Vík. Vík is the southernmost village in Iceland, famous for it's black sand beach Dyrholaey.The white foamy was wash up onto the pitch black vocano sand, is truely a sight that you don't see any where else.

Vík to Höfn

The Warmest Coast

Following the Route #1, our next stop is Höfn. Höfn is a small fishing town on the warmenst coast of Iceland. Driving on the coast remineded us of Highway 1 in CA. When you get to Höfn, don't miss out on seeing the colorful fishing boats by the see.

People at Höfn enjoy hot spring pools a lot. Even in the coldest winter there are still public bathes open where people can go and take a hot bath and watch the stars. Our hostess at the guest house told us that somewhere in the moutains there is a nautral hot spring pool. If you ever visit, don't miss it.
